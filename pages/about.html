About the Project

Target Audiences

Primary Audience: DIA Museum Visitors

General public visitors (200,000+ annually at DIA) seeking deeper understanding of artworks across all collections—African, Asian, European, American, Contemporary, Indigenous, Oceanic, and Islamic art

Lifelong learners and art enthusiasts interested in connecting artworks to broader historical, social, and cultural contexts beyond traditional Western-centric museum narratives

Critical theory-engaged visitors looking for decolonized interpretations that challenge dominant narratives, center marginalized perspectives, and reveal power dynamics in art historical frameworks

K-12 and university students on educational visits requiring accessible, multi-layered content for varying knowledge levels across diverse cultural collections

Diaspora and heritage communities (African American, Asian American, Latino/a, Indigenous, Arab American, and other communities) seeking authentic representations of their cultural heritage throughout the museum

Data, Information and Content

How We Show What We Know (And What We're Still Learning)

We use a color-coded system to be transparent about our curatorial process:

    Blue: Basic information to understand the object
    Green: Objects with interesting geographic stories or migration histories
    Orange: Objects with mixed cultural backgrounds or cross-cultural influences
    Red: We're actively rethinking how we frame these objects
    Yellow: Waiting on input from cultural experts or community members

The Tech Side

We're using tools that are either free or low-cost for educational and cultural institutions:

    For Creating 3D Models: Reality Capture (free for schools) and Maya (about $235/year for education, sometimes free through universities)
    For Displaying Objects: Sketchfab (free for museums) and MetaAR for the AR experience
    For Mapping Connections: Cytoscape (free and open-source)

The Platform: A website that works on any device, with cloud storage that can grow as we add more institutions People just visit reframingcollections.org on any browser and everything works—no app downloads or special software needed.

What It Costs

    Most of the software we're using is free for educational and cultural institutions. The real costs are people's time and cloud hosting. Starting Small (1-2 museums, 50-100 objects): $15,000-30,000 A part-time team working with a couple of partner institutions to prove the concept works.
